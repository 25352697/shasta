Motivation

De novo assembly for human genomes from Oxford Nanopore reads was first demonstrated by Jain et al., 2018, but at substantial computational cost and complexity. The goal of the Shast project is to reduce this computational cost and complexity to the point of enabling routine and/or clinical de novo assembly for human genomes. For this, the assembler must be:

  1. Fast: under one day elapsed time.
  2. Logistically simple and easy to run.
  3. Accurate: accuracy and other assembly metrics must be comparable to those provided by existing tools. Assembly accuracy should be limited by data quality, not by assembly algorithms.

The Shasta assembler has fulfilled the first two goals. An evaluation is in process regarding the third goal, but early indications are that Shasta accuracy is at least comparable to alternative, less performant assemblers. See here for more information on current status.
